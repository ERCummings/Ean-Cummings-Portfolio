About

My Story

My passion for engineering began with a simple desire: to help people live healthier lives. While I initially thought that meant becoming a doctor, my curiosity about how things work led me down a different path. The moment I saw the rise of fitness trackers like the Apple Watch, I had an epiphany. If a device could motivate someone to walk 10,000 steps, what could an even smarter device do? My goal is to use technology to empower individuals, providing them with affordable, accessible insights to prevent illness and give them a deeper understanding of their own health.

My Philosophy

I believe that opportunity favors the bold. If you never ask, the answer is always no. This is what drives me as an entrepreneur and maker—to take a chance on an idea and see it through. It's also why I value a great work ethic above all else. While technical skill is important, I believe a hardworking, eager-to-learn mindset is the most valuable asset in any team. I'm always ready to ask for help when I need it and to learn from the people around me.

EKG

Built a working EKG to monitor heart rate and detect irregular rhythms for a class project.

For a class project, I designed and built a working EKG (electrocardiogram) from the ground up, with the objective of monitoring heart rate and identifying irregular rhythms. The device's circuit included a custom-designed **instrumentation amplifier** and a series of **active filters** (low-pass and high-pass) to capture a clean signal from electrode sensors. A key technical challenge was mitigating 60 Hz electrical noise; I solved this by implementing a **notch filter** in the code to ensure accurate data acquisition. The project successfully demonstrated real-time heart rate detection and irregular rhythm analysis, confirming the design's effectiveness.

InsulTrack — Smart Insulin Pen Case

I co-led a capstone project that evolved into a company, designing an all-in-one smart insulin pen case and companion app to solve a critical user need.

My team and I were frustrated by the lack of a comprehensive solution on the market for people who rely on insulin. Current devices were fragmented—some offered dosage tracking, others provided thermal protection, but none addressed all the critical needs of users. Our goal was to create a single device that combines thermal insulation, GPS tracking, dosage logging, and impact protection.

The biggest challenge was bridging the gap between different engineering disciplines. We had to seamlessly integrate a complex physical device with its intricate circuitry, then program its hardware to work with a companion app. This process taught me a fundamental lesson: engineering isn't just about what you learn in a textbook, it's about the iterative, real-world process of getting something wrong and going back to the drawing board.

To provide a full solution, we developed a companion iOS app that communicates with the pen case via Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE). The app provides users with valuable, real-time insights by logging insulin usage, blood glucose levels, food intake, and exercise. This holistic approach empowers users to better understand their body's trends and make informed decisions about their health.

This project was so successful that we earned 3rd place in the Hubert E. Storer Engineering Student Entrepreneurial Start-up Award Competition and are in the process of forming an LLC.

DNA/Protein Extraction

Applied molecular biology and bioinformatics to isolate and analyze DNA and proteins from cell cultures.

This lab-based project was an opportunity to apply my knowledge of both biomedical science and data analysis. I successfully performed a series of core molecular biology techniques, including **DNA and protein isolation**, **spectrophotometry** to quantify protein concentration, and **gel electrophoresis** to separate and analyze the samples. While some of the lab runs yielded imperfect results, it taught me the importance of meticulous technique and troubleshooting in a scientific environment.

The core of this project was using **Python and bioinformatics** to make sense of the results. I wrote custom scripts to analyze the gel images and perform **sequence alignment** using the **Needleman-Wunsch algorithm** to compare genetic relationships between samples. I also developed code to translate DNA sequences into proteins, demonstrating a comprehensive understanding of computational biology principles.

Healthur — Personal Health & Recovery Coach

An iOS app that synthesizes data like HRV, activity, and sleep into a daily health score.

I'm developing Healthur to address a critical gap in the health app market: the lack of truly personalized, actionable insights. While many apps collect vast amounts of data, they often hide valuable information behind paywalls or present it in a way that’s difficult for users to understand.

My goal is to provide a comprehensive, free AI-powered assistant that gives users a "Smart Health Score" based on their HRV, sleep, and activity data. The app will meet users wherever they are on their health journey, offering simple, achievable steps and highlighting trends in their health and recovery. For me, privacy is a non-negotiable. I'm building Healthur with a strong emphasis on on-device processing to ensure that no personal health data is ever shared with external servers.

The biggest challenge so far has been developing an accurate and adaptable AI model. To make the AI truly helpful, it needs to analyze a wide range of data points to provide genuinely personalized recommendations. This is an ongoing process of data analysis and model training to ensure the AI's suggestions are effective and trustworthy.
